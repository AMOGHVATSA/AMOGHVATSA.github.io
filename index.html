EU and India Relations

Posted on, Novemeber 20, 2021

India is one of the fastest growing economies in the world on the verge of becoming a global superpower. Europe is a major market for trading and business. To develop, India need the European Union’s help and European Union needs India’s help to stronger its hold on the Asian/Indian market. The current relations between EU and India are great. They have been great since 1994 after the Cooperation agreement. India is currently EU’s largest trade partner contributing to 12.5% of India’s overall trades between 2015 and 2016. India is one of the nations that have a surplus amount of trades with the EU. EU and India are not only major trading partners they have had major cooperations in nuclear energy, environment protection, maritime security, climate change just to name a few. Both the sides are also contributing to data protection and regulation, India and EU have worked together in developing ICT standardization as well as having talks regarding ethnic use of AI and 5G. India had also helped EU with its satellite navigation system Galileo. The current conditions are indicating a bright future of India and EU relations. Once again I will repeat my statement the cooperation between EU and India is required for both of the parties to advance forward and considering the great business involvement and profitable trade this partnership is one to last.

India and Sweden relations

Posted on, Novemeber 20, 2021

The ties between Sweden and India run deep. India has set many diplomatic missions in Sweden. Both the countries have made public their deep admiration for their economic and cultural relations. This partnership has strong uprising in the past couple of years due to many bilateral visits. Sweden and India have had many collaborations in many fields. Trades in goods and services has increased from 3 billion USD to 4.5 billion USD and isn’t showing any signs of slowing down. There are many companies in Sweden having great presence and vice versa. The range of areas of cooperation include : Smart Cities & CleanTech, Nano Sciences, Computer Sciences, AI, Smart Grids, Energy Research (storage, e-mobility, new fuels), water, waste to wealth, circular economy, embedded systems, new technologies. Other areas include : Digitisation and Internet of Things (IOT), Health & Life Sciences, Biomedical Devices, Bio-Economy, Test Beds, Digital Healthcare, Ageing, New materials, Space & Aeronautics among many other. India is the third largest Asian trading partner of Sweden. The cooperation between India and Sweden is a long running partnership is a bond which is unlikely to be broken in the near future this collaboration has a long way to go and could be one to help India finally develop into a global superpower.
